FAQs

 

Compensation & Pension (C&P) Exams?

You will have to attend C&P exams after we submit your claim. Everyone wants to know how these will go, but there is no set script. It’s our policy not to give advice on C&P exams, and we’ll advise you not to go looking on the internet for C&P exam tips either, because the self-proclaimed “experts” on YouTube and Facebook will stress you out. Just be on time to your exam.

How much does this cost?

It varies. We charge an initial amount for handling your application, and a recurring amount for handling all the follow-up paperwork with the VA. We’ve filed enough claims to make the process as simple as possible for you, and we know our service will pay for itself pretty quickly.

Are you a VA Lawyer?

Nope! “VA Lawyers” are attorneys who’ve decided been approved to represent veterans for compensation claims and before the VA Board of Appeals. You can think of them as someone who you give “Power of Attorney” to so they can handle your case. The problem with VA attorneys is that they are restricted to very specific VA guidelines when they help you. These guidelines lower the amount they can charge, ultimately taking away the incentive for attorneys to help veterans.

Are you a Veteran Services Officer(“VSO'“)?

Nope! VSO’s are similar to attorneys in that they are heavily regulated and poorly incentivized to help veterans. Many of them are volunteers, who process claims for free. While this may sound great, it doesn’t work well in practice, because VSO’s aren’t getting paid, so they can burn out quickly. Can you blame them?

Who are you then?

We are veterans, but we are also engineers that write software to automate and optimize government processes.

Why are you doing this?

We completed our own VA Claims, and we were blown away by how broken the process is. The VA Claims system is chaos. The VA G.I. Bill process works smoothly though. Why? Because private colleges are incentivized to make the G.I. Bill process simple for veterans and the VA, so they can accept the G.I. Bill, enroll student veterans, and make more money. Private companies find a way to get it right when there is an opportunity. As a company, we believe if we give every veteran a great experience in the VA Claims process, then we’ll deserve the money we make. So it’s our goal to make the VA Claims process simple. For all of us.
